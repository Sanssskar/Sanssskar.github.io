About Me

Full Stack Developer | Freelancer ⚔️

👋 Hey there! I'm Sanskar Shrestha — a web developer from Dharan, Nepal. I recently wrapped up my Bachelor's in Information Management (BIM) and have been deep in the freelance world ever since.

💻 Been coding for about 2 years now. Mostly Laravel and Filament, but I've built everything from e-commerce stores to student portals and REST APIs. Nothing beats that feeling when a client says "this is exactly what I wanted" after weeks of building something from scratch.

🎓 College days: BIM wasn't just about grades for me — it gave me a solid mix of tech and business. Understanding databases is cool, but knowing why a client needs a feature is what makes a project actually work. That balance helps me not just code, but solve real problems.

💼 Freelance life: Started small in 2022, building whatever came my way. Now I'm working with clients from Nepal and abroad. Learned the hard way that clear communication matters more than perfect code sometimes. Still, meeting deadlines and making clients happy is what keeps me going. I'm open for new projects if something interesting comes along.

🎯 How I work: Before writing any code, I spend time understanding what the client actually needs. Sounds obvious, but you'd be surprised how often people jump straight into building the wrong thing. I make sure what I build actually solves the problem, not just looks pretty.

⚔️ When I'm not coding: You'll probably find me somewhere in the hills around Dharan with my headphones on, or binge-watching anime when I should be sleeping. Gotta keep that balance, you know? Coding burns you out if you don't step away sometimes.

🌱 What's next: Currently diving into Vue.js and figuring out Laravel Forge deployments. Never really stop learning in this field — that's the fun part, honestly.

My Freelancing Journey

How It All Started (2023)

It was summer, and I had this college project coming up. Needed to build something that worked both frontend and backend — had no clue where to start. Then a friend introduced me to Laravel. We sat together, he showed me the basics, and honestly? I got hooked. Built my first project by learning alongside him, and somewhere along the way, coding stopped being just an assignment and became something I actually enjoyed.

The Addiction Phase

After that first project, I couldn't stop. Started helping friends with their projects — small websites, fixing bugs, adding features. Every time something worked, it felt like a small win. The more I built, the more I wanted to learn. That's when I realized this wasn't just a one-time thing anymore.

Joining CodeIT Nepal

Wanted to take things seriously, so I reached out to CodeIT Nepal. Got in, learned proper workflows, version control, real-world project structures — stuff you don't pick up from random YouTube tutorials. The mentorship there changed how I approached coding. Suddenly, my messy code started making sense to other people too.

Where I Am Now (2025)

From that summer project to now — been freelancing full-time, mostly Laravel and Filament work. Built projects like Home Link Services, Koseli Express, Student Portal API. Still learning every day, still messing up sometimes, but that's part of the game. If you've got something interesting to build, hit me up. Would love to help out.
